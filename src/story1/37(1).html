Why This WORKS

  • Validates Her Identity and History
    • "You've been taking care of yourself and me for years" honors her lifetime of competence
    • Connects to her identity as a capable caregiver and mother
    • Shows respect for who she has always been, not just who she is now
  • Reframes the Relationship
    • "I'm just here if you want company" changes dynamic from helper-helpee to companion
    • Makes Sarah's presence a choice rather than an imposition
    • Preserves Amelia's sense of control and autonomy
  • Uses Non-Confrontational Language
    • "Of course" validates rather than argues with her perspective
    • Agrees with her reality instead of correcting it
    • Harvard research emphasizes "don't confront" - this approach follows that guidance
  • Offers Support Without Pressure
    • "If you want" gives her complete control over accepting help
    • No demands, commands, or expectations placed on her
    • Shows care without creating obligation or stress
  • Maintains Dignity and Self-Esteem
    • Treats her as a capable adult who can make decisions
    • Preserves her personhood and sense of worth
    • Research shows "maintain their dignity and self-esteem" is crucial
