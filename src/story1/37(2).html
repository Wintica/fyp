Why This DOESN'T Work

  • Direct Confrontation with Memory Loss
    • Lists specific failures ("forgot breakfast," "can't find clothes") which creates shame
    • Forces Amelia to confront her deficits directly, causing distress and anxiety
    • Research shows confronting memory loss increases agitation and behavioral symptoms
  • Argumentative Approach
    • "Yes, you DO need help!" creates a power struggle and defensive response
    • Turns the interaction into a win-lose battle rather than collaboration
    • Harvard research shows confrontation "avoid[s] a standoff" and should be avoided
  • Memory Testing Through Examples
    • Listing forgotten tasks forces her to acknowledge memory failures
    • Creates learned helplessness and depression about her condition
    • Violates the cardinal rule: "Do not test the memory of persons living with dementia"
  • Overwhelming Information
    • Multiple examples at once overwhelm her processing ability
    • Creates cognitive overload when she's already struggling
    • Goes against guidance to "keep sentences short and simple"
Consequence: Damages Trust & Communication
