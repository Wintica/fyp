Why This WORKS

  • Starts with Affirmation
    • "Mom, you look nice today" immediately validates her and preserves dignity
    • Creates positive emotional tone before any requests
    • Shows respect for her as a person
  • Uses Collaborative Language
    • "Should we choose together" makes it a partnership, not a command
    • Maintains her agency in decision-making
    • Shows she's still capable of making choices
  • Offers Specific, Manageable Choices
    • "Soft gray one or your favorite cardigan" gives clear options
    • Reduces decision fatigue common in dementia
    • Focuses on comfort and preferences, not appearance standards
  • Adds Personal Touch
    • "With the pretty buttons" shows attention to details she might appreciate
    • Connects to her sense of style and aesthetics
    • Makes the choice feel special rather than clinical
  • Research-Backed Effectiveness
    • Choice-giving maintains sense of autonomy and dignity
    • Positive framing improves cooperation and mood
